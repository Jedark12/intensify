Phishing Awareness

Be cautious of suspicious emails, messages, or links that ask for personal information. Verify the sender's legitimacy and avoid clicking on unknown links or downloading attachments from unfamiliar sources.

Regular Software Updates

Keep all your devices and software up-to-date with the latest security patches. Updates often contain fixes for vulnerabilities that hackers may exploit.

Privacy Settings and Permissions

Review and adjust privacy settings on social media platforms and other online accounts to control what information is shared and with whom. Be mindful of the permissions granted to apps and services, allowing only necessary access to your data.

Online safety awareness

Identity Theft

Cybercriminals may steal personal information such as social security numbers, credit card details, or login credentials, leading to potential financial loss or unauthorized access to accounts.

Phishing Attacks

Cyber attackers often use deceptive emails or websites to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information or installing malware, compromising their data security.

Cyberbullying

Online platforms can be breeding grounds for harassment, intimidation, or malicious behavior, impacting individuals' mental health and well-being in the virtual space.
